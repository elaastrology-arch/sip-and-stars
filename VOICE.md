# sip & stars: Brand Voice Guide

## Brand Personality

If sip & stars were a person: the friend who happens to be a professional astrologer and always somehow has time for you over coffee. She explains your chart the way she'd explain a great book she just finished, genuinely excited, plain-spoken, no gatekeeping, no incense-and-crystals theater.

## Voice Attributes

**Warm & conversational**
- We are: personal, second-person, "pull up a chair"
- We are not: clinical or script-like
- Sounds like: "You bring your birth details and your questions, I bring the chart and the conversation."
- Not: "Schedule a consultation to receive your astrological analysis."

**Plain-spoken**
- We are: accessible to total beginners
- We are not: dumbed-down (the journal goes deep, it just never gatekeeps)
- Sounds like: "The most honest selfie you'll ever take, no camera required."
- Not: "Your natal chart delineates planetary configurations at the moment of parturition."

**Confident, not vague**
- We are: willing to say plainly what astrology is and isn't
- We are not: hedge-everything, "the stars may suggest..." language
- Sounds like: "A birth chart is not a script."

**Playful, metaphor-driven**
- We are: the coffee/pour conceit run all the way through (House Blend, The Refill, "taste the blend")
- We are not: try-hard punny or precious about it

## Messaging Pillars (in priority order)

1. **A practical, daily-life tool.** Astrology isn't just "here's your personality": every reading leaves you with a small, usable action for today. Not abstract insight for its own sake.

2. **A roadmap for when you're lost.** Beyond the daily practice, astrology offers real guidance at life's crossroads, offered without pressure, arriving when you're ready for it, never pushed. *"You don't need a reading today. But if you're here, some part of you is already looking for one."*

3. **Solar return is the specialty.** The Refill, a year-ahead reading calculated from wherever you'll spend your next birthday (not your birth city), is MD's signature offering. This location-specific detail is the hook: it makes the reading feel personal and precisely timed, not generic. *"Tell me where you'll be for your next birthday, and I'll read the year that's about to start from there."*

4. **Self-understanding over fortune-telling.** The chart shows energies and patterns, not a fixed fate. Never sell certainty about the future; sell clarity about yourself.

5. **Warm and human-scale.** One astrologer (MD), the coffee ritual, in person in Tallinn or online. Not a faceless service or an app.

## Audience

Self-understanding-curious adults, 20s–40s, EN/ES bilingual. Split between Tallinn locals and global online clients. Explicitly welcoming to total beginners, not just people already fluent in astrology.

## Tone Spectrum

| | |
|---|---|
| Formality | Casual, conversational (not formal) |
| Authority | Peer-level, explains rather than lectures |
| Emotion | Warm, but matter-of-fact when it counts |
| Complexity | Accessible on the surface, real depth available (journal) |
| Energy | Calm and grounded (not hype-y) |
| Humor | Lightly playful (not jokey) |

## Style Rules

- Use the Oxford comma consistently
- Sentence-case headings, no period on fragments
- Second person ("you") throughout
- Favor commas and parentheticals over em dashes for asides (em dashes are banned sitewide, see CLAUDE.md)
- Reach for the coffee/pour metaphor when it fits naturally, don't force it into every sentence
